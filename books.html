The Death of Wisdom,
the Rise of Folly

Why We Must Care

Dr. Thompson saw America with the image of children playing a seesaw — a single plank, kept going by the pivot in the middle and players on each side pushing up so the player on the other side would be pushed down. He saw America and the world, at every level, like that seesaw game, with Folly on one side and Wisdom on the other. Everywhere he looked, Folly was rising to the top while Wisdom struggled to find her legs and get up. He wanted to give Wisdom a leg up.

In the book, Wisdom is personified as a lady. He wanted her to rise up against the dark forces in America and the world keeping her down. She is described as an abused lady — and we need to come to her aid. Written about six years ago as a prophetic call, today Wisdom appears to be on life support in the back of the train. Dr. Thompson was inspired to write this book to give Wisdom a fighting chance to survive. This is the most urgent call in our times to care before she dies on our watch.

"Wisdom is riding on a passenger train of time on a collision course with destiny, and we are all in for the ride."

The Dark Side of the Gospel

Looking at the Darkness to See the Light

A book about the reality that bad things happen to good people. It explores why — and how we can find solace and salvation in suffering, just as Jesus did.

Deliver Us from Evil

A Prayer for Our Times

Follows the theme of the first book. A deep dive into the spiritual backstage of life, explaining how forces beyond what we see influence what we do. It lays out the biblical spiritual battle plan to contend with the fierce enemy of evil — an evil so dark and fierce that God Himself asked all His followers to pray to be delivered from it even in this life.

The Evangelical Church at the Crossroads of Secular Culture and Change

Facing the Tough Issues of Myth and Mission

A manifesto to the Evangelical Church, addressing the often-destructive ways it has engaged with secular culture in its proclamation of the Gospel. The book outlines major fault lines, identifies myths, distinguishes the Church's biblical mandate, and clarifies its methods consistent with the New Testament church form and freedom Christ left His Church to follow.

Why Do Birds Fly

How to Fly High in a World Trying to Keep You Down

Taking a much lighter tone from the heavy issues of the day, this book is geared to a younger teenage audience. Once focused in the celebrity world of photography, Dr. Thompson turned here to his love of photographing nature — particularly birds — and what wisdom we can gain from their beauty and behavior. His message to youths: fly high in a world often trying to keep you down.

YOUNG ADULT

Spaces of Existence — Three-Volume Work

Inside-out, Outside-in · Me Against the World · We Against the World

Based on a graphic model created by the author to illustrate and explain the comprehensive spaces of the universe in which we all live. The volumes explore the major questions of existence based on his theories, arguing that such theories should not be left solely to biologists, physicists, or AI, which consider only a limited dimension of human existence. Built over some twenty years of research, the works address the central questions: Who Are We, Who Are We Becoming, Who Do We Want to Be? — especially critical in an age of the rise of the machines and the massive influence of artificial intelligence. The folly of our generation, Dr. Thompson argues, would be to leave the definition of who we are to those who never made us: beings who are "a little lower than angels, fearfully and wonderfully made." Still human after all these years.
